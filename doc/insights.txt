SafeTicket - Insights, Ideas & Strategic Notes
=================================================

Collected during co-founder interview, May 2026.
These are non-obvious observations, ideas, and strategic angles
worth remembering as the project evolves.


========================================================================
BRAND & POSITIONING
========================================================================

1. "YOU BUY FROM SAFETICKET, NOT FROM A STRANGER"
   The platform is a store, not a classifieds board. The buyer's
   relationship is with SafeTicket, not with the seller. The seller
   is invisible — a supplier behind the scenes. This is fundamentally
   different from Yad2 or Facebook groups where you deal with a person.
   Every piece of marketing, UI copy, and support interaction should
   reinforce this.

2. THE FOUNDER'S SCAM STORY IS THE BEST MARKETING ASSET
   One co-founder bought a ticket on Facebook, got rejected at the
   gate, seller stopped answering. That anger, that helplessness —
   every person in a Facebook ticket group has felt it or fears it.
   This story should be front and center on the homepage. Not in
   "About Us." On the hero section. Real, short, in Hebrew.
   "I got scammed. Nobody helped. So we built this."

3. ANTI-SCALPING LAW IS A BRAND ADVANTAGE
   Israeli law prohibits selling tickets above face value. Most
   platforms ignore this. SafeTicket enforces it at the database level
   (asking_price <= face_value). This is a trust signal AND a legal
   compliance feature. Marketing angle: "Fair prices, guaranteed by law
   and by us."

4. SAFETICKET IS NOT COMPETING WITH EVENTIM/LEAAN
   Those are primary market (organizer → buyer). SafeTicket is
   secondary market (buyer → new buyer). They are complementary,
   not competitors. SafeTicket exists because those platforms don't
   offer safe resale. Don't position against them.

5. THE REAL COMPETITOR IS FACEBOOK GROUPS
   People currently sell tickets in Facebook groups like
   "כרטיסים למכירה." The transaction takes 3 minutes: DM, agree,
   Bit/PayBox, screenshot, done. SafeTicket must be safer AND not
   significantly slower than this. If listing a ticket takes 10 minutes,
   sellers go back to Facebook.


========================================================================
OPERATIONAL IDEAS
========================================================================

6. RANDOM PRESENCE AT EVENTS (THE SECURITY CAMERA EFFECT)
   Plan for first 100 events: send a co-founder to physically verify
   that buyers got in. But you don't need to be at EVERY event.
   Going to 1/3 of events is enough — people don't know which events
   you're at. Like security cameras: half can be fake and they still
   work as deterrence. Both buyers and sellers behave honestly because
   they MIGHT be watched.

7. EVENT PRESENCE = SOCIAL MEDIA CONTENT
   Every event you attend produces content:
   - "We were at [event]. All 12 SafeTicket buyers got in!"
   - Photos/stories of happy customers at the gate
   - Behind-the-scenes of the verification process
   This is authentic content, not ads. It builds trust AND awareness
   simultaneously. Free marketing that also serves an operational purpose.

8. PUSH NOTIFICATIONS FOR ADMIN REVIEW
   At MVP volume (few listings per day), all three co-founders should
   receive a push notification (email) when a new listing is submitted.
   Whoever is free handles it. This minimizes review time without
   requiring a formal on-call schedule.

9. SUPPORT VIA GMAIL IS FINE FOR MVP
   Contact form messages appear in admin panel. Reply via
   support@safeticket.co.il in Gmail. At 5-10 messages per week,
   building an in-app reply system is overengineered. Switch when
   volume justifies it.


========================================================================
GROWTH INSIGHTS
========================================================================

10. THE SUPPLY SIDE CREATES ITSELF
    Organizers spend money marketing events. Those campaigns drive
    ticket purchases on Leaan/Eventim. Some of those buyers can't
    attend and need to resell. SafeTicket's supply of tickets is
    generated by marketing spend that SafeTicket doesn't pay for.
    You just need to be where people think of when they can't go.

11. FACEBOOK GROUPS ARE THE HUNTING GROUND
    Your first sellers are currently in Facebook groups trying to
    sell tickets to strangers. That's where you find them. But be
    careful — posting promotional content in those groups may get
    you banned. Approach might be: engage naturally, share the scam
    story, let people discover SafeTicket.

12. SUMMER IS LAUNCH SEASON
    Israel's event season peaks in summer (June-September): outdoor
    concerts, festivals, park shows. This is when ticket resale
    demand is highest. Launching before summer maximizes the chance
    of early traction.

13. SELLER FRICTION IS THE BIGGEST RISK
    The seller is already losing money (selling at or below face value).
    They also lose 10% commission. They also wait until after the event
    for payout. The ONLY reason to use SafeTicket over Facebook is
    safety and convenience. The listing process must be as fast and
    painless as possible. Three steps max: pick event, upload ticket,
    set price.

14. WORD OF MOUTH FROM SUCCESSFUL TRANSACTIONS
    Every successful transaction produces two happy people: a buyer who
    got in safely and a seller who got paid. Both will tell friends.
    At early stage, this organic growth is more valuable than paid ads.
    Consider asking happy users for testimonials / permission to share
    their story.


========================================================================
TECHNICAL INSIGHTS
========================================================================

15. ESCROW VIA PSP, NOT SAFETICKET
    SafeTicket must NEVER hold money directly. Route all payments
    through a licensed Payment Service Provider (Stripe, PayPlus,
    Tranzila). The PSP holds escrow, handles AML compliance, and
    carries the regulatory burden. This keeps SafeTicket out of
    financial services regulation. This is a legal architecture
    decision, not just a technical one.

16. GUEST CHECKOUT IS LEGALLY VIABLE
    Israeli law does not require buyer account creation for online
    purchases. Minimum data needed: name (for receipt), email
    (for ticket delivery), phone (for SMS link). Privacy law
    actually FAVORS collecting less data (data minimization principle).

17. EVENT TICKETS ARE EXEMPT FROM 14-DAY CANCELLATION RIGHT
    Section 14G(d)(4) of Israeli Consumer Protection Law exempts
    entertainment for a specific date. Buyers cannot legally demand
    a refund simply because they changed their mind. Must be clearly
    disclosed before purchase.

18. PWA BEFORE APP STORE
    A Progressive Web App gives mobile users an app-like experience
    (home screen icon, full-screen, offline caching) with zero
    platform fees. App Store / Play Store come later, with web
    checkout to avoid the 30% Apple/Google commission.

19. OCR FOR PRICE VERIFICATION (FUTURE)
    Currently admin manually reads face value from ticket files.
    OCR can automate this: extract price from ticket image, compare
    to seller's asking price, auto-approve if compliant. Significant
    time saver as volume grows.

20. AI EVENT MONITORING (FUTURE)
    Scrape Israeli ticketing websites (Leaan, Eventim, Kupat Rashut,
    venue sites) twice daily to detect new events. Auto-add to
    SafeTicket's event database. Eliminates the manual event-adding
    bottleneck.


========================================================================
DEFENSIVE NOTES
========================================================================

21. THE 99% ASSUMPTION IS YOUR BUSINESS MODEL
    The platform is built on the premise that most people are honest.
    The escrow timing (money held until after event) means fraud costs
    SafeTicket nothing financially — you just refund from escrow.
    The platform only fails if fraud becomes so common that trust
    collapses. Monitor fraud rate closely. If it exceeds 2-3%,
    tighten controls. If it goes above 5%, rethink the model.

22. BOTH-SIDES FRAUD EXISTS
    Buyer fraud (friendly fraud): attend event, claim ticket didn't
    work, get refund = free ticket. Seller fraud: sell fake ticket
    or duplicate ticket. Random event presence deters both sides
    without either knowing when they're being watched.

23. IDENTITY AS DETERRENCE, NOT PREVENTION
    Seller verification (ID, phone, bank account) doesn't prevent
    fraud — it makes fraud traceable. A scammer knows you have their
    real identity. Combined with low ticket values (100-500 ILS) and
    the effort required, the risk/reward ratio makes scamming not
    worth it for most people.

24. COMPLETE SEPARATION BETWEEN BUYER AND SELLER
    Neither party knows the other's identity. Ever. This:
    - Protects privacy
    - Prevents platform bypass (can't do direct deals next time)
    - Simplifies dispute resolution (only SafeTicket arbitrates)
    - Makes SafeTicket the indispensable middleman
