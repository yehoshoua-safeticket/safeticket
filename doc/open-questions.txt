SafeTicket - Open Questions
=============================

Questions the co-founders need to discuss, research, or get
professional advice on before launch. Organized by priority.


========================================================================
MUST RESOLVE BEFORE LAUNCH (Blockers)
========================================================================

Q1: FINANCIAL SERVICES CLASSIFICATION
    Does SafeTicket's escrow model classify the platform as a
    "payment service provider" under Israeli law?
    ACTION: Consult an Israeli fintech/payments attorney.
    If YES: you need a license from the Bank of Israel.
    If NO (because PSP holds the money): you're clear.
    This is the single most important legal question.
    COST: ~1,000-2,000 ILS for a legal consultation.

Q2: WHICH PSP TO USE?
    Options: Stripe, PayPlus, Tranzila, PayMe.
    Questions to ask each provider:
    - Do you support escrow / delayed capture / hold-and-release?
    - What are your per-transaction fees?
    - Can you hold funds for weeks (until after the event)?
    - Do you handle AML/KYC on behalf of the platform?
    - What's the onboarding process for a new חברה בע"מ?
    - Do you support payouts to third-party bank accounts (sellers)?
    ACTION: Contact 2-3 PSPs and compare. This choice affects
    your entire payment architecture.

Q3: FOUNDERS AGREEMENT
    Must be signed BEFORE registering the חברה בע"מ.
    Must include:
    - Ownership split (33/33/33 agreed)
    - Vesting schedule (recommend 4 years with 1-year cliff)
    - What happens if a co-founder leaves
    - What happens if co-founders disagree (2 vs 1 = majority?)
    - IP assignment (who owns the code?)
    - Non-compete during and after
    - What constitutes "cause" for removal
    ACTION: Find a startup lawyer. Budget ~3,000-5,000 ILS.

Q4: TERMS OF SERVICE & PRIVACY POLICY
    Required before accepting any users or payments.
    Must include:
    - SafeTicket as intermediary, not seller
    - Cancellation policy (exempt per Section 14G(d)(4))
    - Dispute resolution process
    - Seller obligations (accurate listing, valid ticket, no cancellation after sale)
    - Commission structure clearly stated
    - Data collection and retention policy
    - Hebrew language (mandatory for Israeli consumers)
    ACTION: Lawyer drafts this. Can often be bundled with founders agreement.

Q5: COMPANY REGISTRATION
    Register חברה בע"מ with the Israeli Registrar of Companies.
    Need: company name, registered address, directors, articles of association.
    ACTION: Lawyer or accountant can handle. ~1,500 ILS.
    Must be done before PSP onboarding.


========================================================================
SHOULD RESOLVE BEFORE LAUNCH (Important)
========================================================================

Q6: WHAT IF THE TICKET FILE LEAKS?
    Buyer receives the ticket file immediately. They could share it
    with friends. Now multiple people have the same ticket file.
    - Can you watermark the file? (adds buyer's name/order number)
    - Can you convert to a SafeTicket-branded ticket? (wrap the
      original in a new PDF with your branding + order reference)
    - Or do you accept this risk as part of the 99% trust model?
    DISCUSS: Is this a real risk or a theoretical one?

Q7: WHAT ABOUT PHYSICAL TICKETS?
    MVP supports digital tickets only. But some events still use
    physical paper tickets (especially smaller venues, box office sales).
    - Do you turn away sellers with physical tickets?
    - Or do you plan a future flow (courier, meetup at venue)?
    DISCUSS: How many potential sellers are lost by excluding physical?

Q8: DUPLICATE LISTING ACROSS PLATFORMS
    A seller lists the same ticket on SafeTicket AND in a Facebook group.
    Ticket sells on Facebook first. Seller cancels on SafeTicket (allowed
    before sale). No harm done.
    But: what if the ticket sells on SafeTicket AND Facebook simultaneously?
    Seller can't cancel on SafeTicket (already sold). Two buyers have
    the same ticket. One gets rejected at the gate.
    - Terms of service can forbid this, but enforcement is hard.
    - Is this covered by the escrow refund? (Buyer gets money back)
    DISCUSS: Is a warning in terms of service enough, or do you need
    a technical solution?

Q9: HOW DO YOU VERIFY SELLER IDENTITY?
    MVP: seller uploads ID + selfie. Admin reviews manually.
    - What exactly does the admin check? Name matches? Photo matches?
    - Do you store the ID image? For how long? (Privacy law implications)
    - Is a selfie holding the ID required, or just the ID alone?
    DISCUSS: Define the exact verification checklist for admins.

Q10: ACCOUNTANT
     A חברה בע"מ requires monthly bookkeeping and annual tax filing.
     - Find an accountant familiar with marketplace/platform businesses
     - They need to understand the escrow model (money flowing through
       is not all revenue — only the commission is)
     ACTION: Get referrals. Budget ~300-500 ILS/month.


========================================================================
SHOULD RESOLVE EARLY ON (After Launch)
========================================================================

Q11: WHAT IF AN EVENT IS CANCELLED?
     Tickets were sold on SafeTicket, money is in escrow, event gets
     cancelled by the organizer.
     - Auto-refund all buyers for that event?
     - Who monitors event cancellations? (Admin manually for MVP)
     - Does the seller get anything? (No — they'll get refund from
       original purchase)
     DISCUSS: Process and communication plan for cancellations.

Q12: WHAT IF AN EVENT IS RESCHEDULED?
     Similar to cancellation but trickier:
     - Buyer might be able to attend the new date (no refund needed)
     - Buyer might NOT be able to attend (wants refund)
     - Seller's ticket might still be valid for new date
     DISCUSS: Policy for rescheduled events.

Q13: WHAT ABOUT MULTIPLE TICKETS IN ONE LISTING?
     Seller has 3 tickets together (friends group, can't all go).
     - Can they list as a bundle? (3 tickets, must buy all 3)
     - Or must each ticket be listed separately?
     - Bundle: simpler for seller, but buyer might only need 2
     - Separate: more flexible, but more admin work (3 reviews)
     DISCUSS: MVP approach.

Q14: HOW DO YOU HANDLE EVENTS WITH SAME NAME, DIFFERENT DATES?
     Example: "Idan Raichel" performs 3 nights in a row at the same venue.
     The event database needs: name + date + venue as unique identifier.
     A seller must pick the exact date, not just the event name.
     DISCUSS: Is the admin data entry process clear enough to avoid
     confusion?

Q15: INSURANCE
     If something catastrophic happens (data breach exposing seller IDs,
     PSP goes bankrupt with escrow money, platform bug causes wrong payouts):
     - Is there insurance for this? (Cyber insurance, professional liability)
     - At what stage does it make sense to get insured?
     DISCUSS: Research costs vs. risks. Probably not needed at MVP
     but worth knowing about.

Q16: WHAT IF SAFETICKET SHUTS DOWN?
     Money is in escrow for upcoming events. Platform shuts down.
     What happens to that money?
     - PSP holds it, not SafeTicket. So it's safe from SafeTicket bankruptcy.
     - But who triggers the release/refund if SafeTicket isn't operating?
     - Terms of service should address this.
     DISCUSS: Include a wind-down clause in terms of service.


========================================================================
STRATEGIC QUESTIONS (Think About Over Time)
========================================================================

Q17: WHEN TO HIRE?
     Three co-founders with side jobs. At what point does the volume
     require hiring? Possible first hires:
     - Part-time admin reviewer (when listing volume exceeds what
       3 people can handle in evenings)
     - Freelance event verifiers (when you can't cover enough events)
     - Customer support person (when Gmail isn't enough)
     THINK: What metric triggers each hire?

Q18: WHEN TO RAISE MONEY?
     Current model is bootstrapped (split costs 3 ways).
     Investment might be needed for:
     - Marketing spend to grow beyond organic
     - Hiring
     - Building the mobile app
     - International expansion
     THINK: At what traction level do you approach investors?
     (Number of transactions, revenue, growth rate)

Q19: INTERNATIONAL EXPANSION
     Israel first. Then where?
     - Same concept works anywhere (ticket fraud is universal)
     - Each country has different consumer protection laws
     - Language, payment methods, event landscape all differ
     - France? (co-founder connection?) US? Europe?
     THINK: What makes a good second market?

Q20: REVENUE DIVERSIFICATION
     Currently: commission only (10% each side).
     Future possibilities:
     - Featured listings (seller pays to appear at top)
     - Event analytics for organizers (anonymous data about resale patterns)
     - Seller subscription (unlimited listings for monthly fee)
     - Buyer alerts ("notify me when tickets for X become available")
     THINK: Which of these aligns with the trust-first brand?

Q21: WHAT IF A COMPETITOR APPEARS?
     Someone builds the same thing in Israel.
     Your moat is:
     - First mover (if you launch soon)
     - Trust / brand recognition
     - Seller and buyer base (network effects)
     - Real stories / social proof from event presence
     THINK: How do you build defensibility beyond just features?
